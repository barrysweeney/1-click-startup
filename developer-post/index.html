Developer Guide

Product Vision

The end product is a system targeted at a business owner which will setup all the services (file sharing, chat, etc) needed to run their business with a single click.

Employees of the business can use the product to help perform their job, log important information, or otherwise interact with colleagues and management.

The product will feature a dashboard, exclusive to the manager, that will allow them to track key productivity metrics and highlight employee engagement.

In this manner, we are digitising the bureaucracy of an organization.

Project Vision

The end project will be the technical implication of the product (as described in the vision) using tools such as Docker, shell scripts, and a Flask REST API webserver.

In addition to this, we also want to provide a testing framework with simulated users and managers using a tool such as PySelenium.

User Experience

We know the what and the how, but our picture is incomplete without understanding who we're developing our product for.

We begin with research to discover our target users and then analyze the gathered data to help define the vision of our product. We then take a user-centric approach to design which we then validate.

Without further ado, let's dive into each of these to see where this missing puzzle piece fits in with regards to the project.

Research

We want to solve problems our user is facing and improve their overall work-flow. In order to do that we need to define our user and figure out more specific details regarding their current work-flows, including the services and platforms they use. We'll also take a look at the approaches being employed by others with similarities to our product.

User Interviews

Research methods can be categorised as either qualitative (open ended questions) or quantitative (close ended questions).

In depth interviews fall on the qualitative side of the line and tend to garner information regarding users' attitudes.

Surveys

In contrast to in-depth interviews, surveys, especially those with no follow-up questions provide quantitative data from which we can analyze responses to recognize patterns.

You can find a report of the survey by opening this PDF file.
